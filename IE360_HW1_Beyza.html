1-Introduction¶

In the last years, Turkey has experienced rising inflation, house prices and exchange rates and these has become hot topic. Since they affect the daily life in many ways, their behaviour is important for citizens' welfare. Also, according to this exchange rate and inflation behavior, people buy or sell gold to protect the value of their money. Firstly, house prices are important in Turkish economy because it is a common practice to buy a house as an investment. On the other hand, due to the fact that many goods especially electronic devices are imported, they are sensetive to any volatility in exchange rates. Finally, inflation means, in general, change in cost of living. Therefore, it affects life quality and welfare of the people. In this study, I tried to explain the relation among parameters mentioned by investigating the correlation and plotting their trends from the data from 2015 to 2021. The data from January 2015 to December 2021 was taken from "Elektronik Veri Datm Sistemi" (EVDS) as monthly and the search data for key word "altn" was taken from Google Trends data.

For exchange rate, US Dollar(Buying); for house prices, "Housing Unit Prices for Turkey - TL/sq m" and finally for inflation, "Consumer Price Index (CPI)" data is used. The aim of the study is to answer the following questions:

  • Is unit housing price sensitive to the fluctuations in the exchange rate?

  • Does increase in the exchange rate means increase in the cost of living which is inflation?

  • Is there really a link between the behavior of inflation and the buying and selling of gold? Could people be buying and selling gold to avoid inflation?

  • Could the increase in inflation be associated with the recent increase in house prices?

When each data is compared in pairs, it is clear that there is a positive correlation between each of these variables, although some have an almost linear relationship and some have a non-linear relationship. Although it is not correct to make a comment about causation here, these 3 parameters and the interest in the price of gold (Here, Google search for the key word "gold" is associated with the demand for gold) are moving together ,generally.My reasoning and inferences for these relations are below:

  • There is two-sided relation between inflation and exchange rates. On one hand, inflation decreases the purchasing power, which results in decrease in the value of money against other currencies. As a result, exchange rate increases with the increase of inflation. On the other hand, Turkey uses most of the imported goods as a intermediary in production. When the cost of importing intermediary goods increases, the cost of final products also increases consequently. This cost increase is reflected in prices, which brings about inflation.

  • Looking at the relationship between exchange rate and house prices, when exchange rates increases, the house price becomes relatively cheaper to the foreigners. This boosts the demand for houses, as a result, house price increases. In addition, with the increase in this exchange rate and depreciation of the currency, the law of granting citizenship to citizens who bought a house for 250 thousand dollars, which was recently enacted has also been a factor in the increase in prices by increasing the demand for house prices significantly.

  • Like home investment, people turn to gold to protect themselves from inflation and the rise of the exchange rate. Therefore, an increase in either of these will increase the demand for gold, thus increasing the price. It is possible that the frequency of Google searches for "gold" will increase with the increase in demand.

6- Conclusion¶

When these correlation values and related plots are examined, it is seen that these parameters are very related to each other and have a linear relationship due to the high correlations. Although many external factors are effective in the increase of one, the effect of the others can be explained to some extent. My answer for the questions in the introduction part with the above observations:

  • Is unit housing price sensitive to the fluctuations in the exchange rate?
    It seems that there is a great correlation between buying prices in USD and house prices. Therefore, house prices may be sensitive to exchange rate and prices may be in line with exchange rate fluctuations.
  • Does increase in the exchange rate means increase in the cost of living which is inflation?
    The fact that the usd buying price is correlated with the consumer price index of 0.98 probably means that when one increases, the other increases.
  • Is there really a link between the behavior of inflation and the buying and selling of gold? Could people be buying and selling gold to avoid inflation?
    Looking to the 0.84 correlation, it can be said that there is a strong linear relationship between inflation and search frequency. When inflation rises, people's willingness to invest in lats and save their money may partly explain the increase in call frequency.
  • Could the increase in inflation be associated with the recent increase in house prices?
    Although a causation relationship between these two cannot be determined by looking at the correlation data alone, it is obvious that the increase of these two is parallel. Although house prices are not included in an inflation package, it is observed that inflation increases when house prices increase.
